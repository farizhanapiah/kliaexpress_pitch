## SLIDE 02 — The Opportunity

**Title:** *Malaysia's Front Door Deserves a Grand Celebration*

**Context points to establish:**
- KLIA Express carries ~15,000–20,000 passengers daily between KL Sentral and KLIA — locals, tourists, and returning Malaysians all passing through
- Malaysia Day 2026 marks 63 years of Malaysian unity — a milestone with strong emotional and patriotic resonance
- KL Sentral and KLIA are the literal arrival/departure point for millions experiencing Malaysia — a high-dwell, high-footfall activation zone
- Competitors and national landmarks will run campaigns; KLIA Express has the unique advantage of a captive, emotionally primed audience (travelers are emotionally heightened — arriving, departing, reuniting)

**The whitespace:** Most transport hubs treat national days as banner-and-bunting affairs. EDT proposes something that makes the station *itself* the experience.

## SLIDE 03 — The Problem We're Solving

**Title:** *Why Traditional Activations No Longer Cut Through*

**Three core tension points:**

**01 — Forgettable Presence**
Static banners and screens get ignored. Commuters tune out. There's no moment of genuine interaction or delight.

**02 — Zero Shareability**
Traditional activations generate no organic social amplification. No one films a flag bunting. KLIA Express misses a massive earned media opportunity.

**03 — No Brand Connection to National Pride**
ERL/KLIA Express is an infrastructure brand. Without a creative experience layer, Malaysia Day becomes a generic campaign that doesn't build brand affinity or long-term recall.

**The result:** Millions of passengers walk through. None of them remember KLIA Express played a role in their Malaysia Day.

## SLIDE 04 — The Big Idea

**Title:** *"Perjalanan Kita" — Our Journey Together*

**The Concept:**
Transform KLIA Express stations (KL Sentral, KLIA) into a living, breathing Malaysia Day experience — where every traveller becomes part of the national story.

**Experience Pillars:**

- **Feel It** — Immersive projection-mapped concourse environments with Malaysia's heritage, landscapes, and cultural icons brought to life
- **Be In It** — AI-powered photobooth (MimpiLab) that transforms passengers into a Merdeka/Malaysia Day hero — branded, shareable, memorable
- **Connect With It** — A Hoomans.ai avatar guide/host stationed at key touchpoints, greeting passengers in Bahasa, English, and Mandarin, sharing Malaysia Day facts and KLIA Express milestones
- **Carry It** — Every participant leaves with a branded AI-generated keepsake photo, shareable to social instantly

**Tone:** Warm, proud, future-forward. Technology that amplifies culture — not replaces it.

## SLIDE 05 — The Experience Design

**Title:** *What It Looks, Feels, and Moves Like*

**Zone 01 — "Gateway of Malaysia" Projection Arc (KL Sentral Concourse)**
A large-format projection-mapped archway or wall displaying a real-time visual journey through Malaysia's 63 years — from Sabah and Sarawak joining the federation, to Malaysia's skyline today. Passengers walk through it. Motion-reactive: footsteps trigger ripples in the visuals.

**Zone 02 — MimpiLab AI Photobooth Station**
2–3 units positioned at KL Sentral and KLIA arrivals/departures hall. Passengers step in, choose a Malaysia Day theme (National Hero, Cultural Icon, Future Malaysian), and receive an AI-generated branded photo in under 30 seconds. Instantly shareable via QR/WhatsApp. KLIA Express watermark on every image.

**Zone 03 — Hoomans.ai Interactive Host**
A holographic AI avatar — dressed in national colours — on an 86" display at key dwell zones. Multilingual. Shares Malaysia Day trivia, KLIA Express history, and encourages participation in the photobooth. Functions as both entertainment and wayfinding.

**Zone 04 — Social Amplification Wall**
A live feed display showing real-time Malaysia Day photos taken at the station, with #PerjalananKita and #KLIAExpress hashtags. Creates a feedback loop of pride and shareability.
